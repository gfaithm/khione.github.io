Procedure Text: Prakarya

Ingredients:

  1. 1 kg of chicken breast fillet
  2. 1 tbsp of garlic powder
  3. 1 tbsp of onion powder
  4. 2 tbsp of black pepper powder
  5. 1,5 cups of wheat flour
  6. 94 grams of cornstarch
  7. Salt to taste
  8. Oil
  9. ⅓ Fish sauce
  10. 1 egg for the marination process and 1 egg for dipping batter
  11. 3 tbsp of sugar
  12. 1 tbsp of paprika powder
  13. 3 tbsp of parmesan cheese
  14. 2 tbsp of vinegar
  15. ¼ tbsp of mushroom bouillon powder

Tools/Equipment:

  1. Bowls
  2. Fork
  3. Plastic wrap
  4. Container (with lid)
  5. Wok
  6. Paper towel
  7. Paper bowl (serving bowl)
  8. Knife
  9. Spatula
  10. Plate

Steps:

  1. Prepare the ingredients and the equipment needed.
  2. Pat the chicken dry with tissues.
  3. Slice the chicken along the grain, and then cut diagonally so it is cube-shaped.
  4. In a bowl, mix one egg, vinegar, and fish sauce.
  5. Add the chicken in the bowl and pierce holes in the chicken with a fork. The bowl is then closed and marinated for a minimum of six hours but not more than twelve.
  6. In a container, mix flour, cornstarch, black pepper powder, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika powder, mushroom bouillon powder, sugar, and salt.
  7. The chicken is then added into the container one at a time and then coated with the seasoning. After it is thoroughly coated, dip the chicken in the bowl of egg and then repeat the seasoning coating process.
  8. Oil is heated to 180 degree Celcius in a large wok. The battered chicken is fried in small batches for 8-12 minutes, depending on the thickness of the chicken meat. The chicken is removed from the wok and placed on a plate covered with a paper towel to rest the chicken.
  9. The snow cheese ingredients are mixed and stirred until smooth.
  10. The cooked chicken is placed on the serving plate or the paper bowl. Snow cheese is sprinkled on top of the chicken.


Procedure Text: Biotechnology

Ingredients:

  1. 1 L of fresh cow milk
  2. 2 tbsp of plain yogurt that contains live bacteria

    3. Tools/Equipment:

    1. Table spoon
    2. Bowl
    3. Saucepan
    4. Spatula
    5. Stove
    6. Container

      7. Steps:

      1. First, the milk should be poured into the pan, then heat the milk in a saucepan over low heat. Stir it constantly until it reaches 85 degrees Celsius. Do not let it boil or burn on the bottom. The protein structure of the milk is changed by heating it over low heat, resulting in a more solid consistency.
      2. After turning off the stove, the milk has to be removed from the heat and cooled by placing the saucepan in a bowl of cold water, stirring constantly, until it reaches 43 degrees Celsius.
      3. Pour the plain yogurt with live cultures into the pan of milk. The yogurt and milk should be mixed together until completely blended.
      4. Transfer the milk mixture to a clean jar. The jar must be closed tightly so that no air can enter, which is important for the fermentation process.
      5. Let the yogurt mixture ferment for 6-7 hours at 38 degrees Celsius. The jar can be placed in a warm place, such as next to the refrigerator. The longer the yogurt ferments, the more acidic it will become and the thicker its texture will be.
      6. After 7 hours or more, the yogurt has to be chilled in the refrigerator for several hours (preferably overnight) before serving. This will help to extend its shelf life by up to 1-2 weeks.
